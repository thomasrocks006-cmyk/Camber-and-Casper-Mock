Core Cultural Traits:
1. Tall Poppy Syndrome
Australians cut down those who stand out
Avoid overt displays of success or superiority
Frame achievements as team efforts
Balance confidence with humility
2. Mateship
Strong emphasis on egalitarianism
"We're all in this together" mentality
Teamwork valued over individual glory
Loyalty and fairness important
3. Directness
Australians are direct but friendly
Prefer straight talk over corporate speak
Value honesty and transparency
Less formal than US/UK
4. Informality
Less hierarchical business culture
First names common, even with CEOs
Casual dress code in many sectors
Meeting structures less rigid
5. Larrikin Spirit
Humor and self-deprecation valued
Not taking oneself too seriously
Playful irreverence acceptable
Authenticity over polish
DISC Type Adaptations:
DOMINANT (D) → High Tall Poppy Risk:
Adaptations:   - Tone down overt displays of success   - Use "we" instead of "I"   - Frame as team achievements, not solo wins   - Balance confidence with humility   - Avoid American-style aggression   - Show you're "one of the team" despite seniority  Phrases to USE:   - "We've had some good wins together..."   - "The team's done well with..."   - "Fair dinkum, the results speak for themselves..."   - "No dramas, we can knock this over quickly..."  Phrases to AVOID:   - "I'm the best..."   - "We're number one..."   - "I've closed deals bigger than this..."   - "Let me tell you how successful I am..."
INFLUENTIAL (I) → Medium Risk:
Adaptations:   - Keep enthusiasm genuine, not salesy   - Use Aussie humor naturally   - Be warm but not over-the-top   - Show you're down-to-earth despite success   - Use storytelling with self-deprecating humor   - Connect through shared experiences, not name-dropping  Phrases to USE:   - "Good on ya mate, that's brilliant..."   - "Yeah, we had a bit of a win there..."   - "How good is that!"   - "She'll be right, we'll sort it out together..."  Phrases to AVOID:   - "OMG this is AMAZING!!!"   - "You're going to LOVE this!!!"   - "This is the BEST thing EVER!!!"   - "Everyone who's anyone uses this..."
STEADY (S) → Low Risk (Natural Fit):
Adaptations:   - Emphasize team and collective success   - Leverage mateship values   - Be reliable and down-to-earth   - Show loyalty and long-term commitment   - Build trust through consistency   - Demonstrate fairness and equality  # S-types naturally align with Australian culture
CONSCIENTIOUS (C) → Low Risk:
Adaptations:   - Maintain professionalism but be approachable   - Don't be overly formal or stuffy   - Show competence without arrogance   - Be thorough but conversational   - Respect their need for detail while keeping it real  # Aussies respect competence but not pompousness
State-Specific Variations:
