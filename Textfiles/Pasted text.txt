1. Proactive AI That Thinks and Acts, Not Just Alerts
You're describing a shift in the AI's role — from informing to executing. This is actually one of the most important design principles we can lock in early, and it changes how Ironbark behaves at its core.

The current spec has Ironbark surfacing insights and asking the owner to act. What you're describing is Ironbark doing the groundwork autonomously and presenting the owner with a ready-made solution — one tap to approve, not ten steps to execute.

The Principle: "Work Prepared, Owner Approves"
Ironbark prepares the outcome. The owner confirms it. The owner never has to solve the problem — just agree that the solution is right.

This is distinct from "smart alerts." It's closer to having a business manager on staff who stays up all night working through your problems and presents you with a recommendation paper in the morning that you can sign off on.

Concrete examples of how this works:
Parts price increase — Ironbark responds proactively:

Detects a price increase from Burson on a commonly ordered part (e.g., Bendix brake pads +14%)
Automatically queries the Repco API for equivalent part pricing
Checks whether you're in a Burson supply contract or on standard trade terms
If no contract lock-in: drafts a switch recommendation — "Repco has equivalent pads at the old price. I've prepared a substitute on your 3 upcoming jobs using this part. Want me to switch the supplier on those and order from Repco?"
If contract lock-in: checks whether a one-off direct purchase from a third supplier (e.g., Rare Spares, a manufacturer distributor) is available at lower cost, or flags that the margin on those jobs needs to be re-quoted
Surfaces one card, two options, no research required from the owner
Cash shortfall — Ironbark responds proactively:

Projects a cash dip 9 days out
Identifies the 3 overdue invoices that, if collected, resolve the gap
Drafts the chase messages — appropriately toned based on how long they've been overdue
Checks if any scheduled discretionary spend (e.g., parts order not yet urgent) can be deferred 5 days without impacting jobs
Presents: "Cash looks tight on the 29th. I've drafted follow-up messages for your 3 overdue invoices ($4,800 total) and flagged a $900 parts order that can be delayed without affecting bookings. Want me to send the messages and hold the order?"

The owner's cognitive load in every case: read, approve, done. Ironbark does the research, the drafting, the cross-checking, and the execution — it just needs the human gate.
